The biometric authentication process

The first thing we do with any player is personally sit down with them to create a biometric fingerprint– a two-factor identification of their voice and signature profile. By doing this, we can verify that it’s that person creating egraphs for fans, no matter where they are in the world.

Getting a thorough biometric fingerprint used in verification is of the upmost importance to us. It’s the basis of the trust both fans and players have in the authenticity of egraphs. >>>>>>> Copy changes to About Us, Landing, Inside Egraphs

Here’s how we create the biometric fingerprint:

Voiceprint

We collect audio samples from each player to create their voiceprint. Our technology extracts pitch, tone, cadence and other voice patterns that get turned into mathematical representations and statistical models. We store these representations in electronic format for use in authentication (see below).

Written signature profile

Like the voiceprint, we collect in-person samples of the player’s signature. Our signature identification technology measures a person’s speed, rhythm, acceleration and movement when they sign their name on an iPad. Similar to the voiceprint, these are stored as complex algorithms for use in authentication (see below).
